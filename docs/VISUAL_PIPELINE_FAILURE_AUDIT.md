# Visual Pipeline Failure Audit — hakcd-v4 v0.1.27

Investigation-only. No fixes applied. No assets regenerated. No code modified.

Auditor: senior pixel-art TD / Playdate graphics specialist (reading the repo cold against professional Playdate reference — Mars After Midnight, Pick Pack Pup, Crankin's Time Travel Adventure, Casual Birder, Saturday Edition, Demon Quest 85).

---

## 1. Executive summary

The game compiles, validates, and ships clean — but it has no visual quality contract anywhere in the pipeline. Every shipped asset originated from one of three failure modes: ImageMagick primitives drawn with `-draw "rectangle ..."`, downscaled-then-1-bit-dithered concept renders that lost everything below 100×60 source resolution, or LDtk levels in which 70% of each room is the same repeated tile. The canon-first migration locked all of this in as "official." The validator now confirms 21/21 PNGs are 1-bit grayscale with detectable outlines — which is true and useless. The art is placeholder-grade. The architecture refuses to admit it.

## 2. Root causes (ranked)

1. **No visual quality contract.** `tools/asset_validator.sh` checks pixel format + edge-pixel count. It does NOT check that an asset is intentionally authored, readable at hardware scale, or compositionally coherent. The "silhouette dump" feature writes images to `build/silhouettes/` but the workflow has no step requiring a human to look at them.
2. **Procedural-primitive sprite as final art.** `tools/sprites/build_newb_from_concept.sh` downscales a 400×240 concept sheet to 16 cells of 32×32 via `-resize 32x32 -filter point -ordered-dither o4x4 -monochrome`. The concept art was 100×60 per cell — losing 90% of pixel detail in the resize. The result reads as blob-with-suggestion-of-hood, not a character. (Confirmed: read `source/images/newb-table-32-32.png` directly — sprites are 9-16 pixel-cluster smudges.)
3. **Rooms are tile-grids with hotspot-prop overlays, not authored scenes.** Both shipped LDtk levels (Bedroom + Playground) are 17×10 = 170 tiles. In each, 120 of those tiles (70.6%) are a single repeated floor tile. Foreground prop layer contains 9-11 placed tiles total. There is no perspective, no depth, no environmental composition. They are debug playgrounds with a wallpaper.
4. **Canon migration promoted placeholder visual language to official.** `source/data/assets.lua` tags `priority='critical'` on the procedural Newb imagetable + the primitive lockpick UI sprite + the carpet-filled LDtk levels. The validator at `tools/canon/validate_continuity.sh` then confirms the entire id graph resolves — locking those placeholders into the contract.
5. **No style guide enforced at build time.** `docs/concept_art_prompt.md` exists and articulates a strong rule set (2px outlines, three-quarter top-down perspective, silhouette must read alone). Zero of those rules are validator-checkable. The prompt is documentation; nothing rejects assets that violate it.

## 4. Character art findings

The Newb 32×32 imagetable is the single most damaging asset in the build. Direct inspection of `source/images/newb-table-32-32.png` (rendered at native 128×224):

- Silhouette readability: **FAIL**. At 32×32 each cell reads as a vertical smudge with a darker top bulge (suggested hood) and a slightly lighter bottom (suggested torso). No head/body/limb distinction. No feet. No hands. Looks like a wax-melted thumbprint.
- Personality: **NONE**. The sprite has no pose intent. Idle and walk are visually indistinguishable except for a 1-pixel offset.
- Pose clarity: **FAIL**. Walk cycle frames differ from idle by sub-frame noise from `-ordered-dither o4x4` not from authored limb motion.
- Animation frame count: 10 states, 26 used cells of 28. Frame distribution is correct (Phase 5 manifest). The art inside the frames is the problem.
- Directional consistency: **PARTIAL**. East-flopped-to-West works (the `-flop` is mechanical). North and south are interchangeable hood smudges with imperceptible front-vs-back differentiation.
- Outline consistency: **NONE**. The dither pass eats outlines on small features. Some frames have a hood outline; others don't.
- Contrast: noisy. Ordered dither at 32×32 destroys the figure/ground separation that a hand-pixel artist would solve with explicit black outline rules.
- Feet anchor stability: **UNKNOWN** at this scale; can't see feet.
- Reads at hardware size: **NO**. On a 400×240 screen at 1:1 pixel ratio, the sprite occupies a 32×32 patch that reads as "small dark thing in the middle." It doesn't read as a person.

**Comparison to professional Playdate reference:** in Mars After Midnight the door-answering protagonist is a hand-drawn ~64×96 character with a clear silhouette, expressive body language, distinct walk-cycle limb articulation, and outline-first rendering. Newb in this build is ~1/9 of that resolution and source-quality. Donald Hays's 32×32 floor rule presumes hand-pixel authoring at the target size — not lossy downscale from concept art.

## 5. Room art findings

Concrete numbers from `source/levels/world.ldtk`:

| Room | Total bg tiles | Most-used tile | Unique tiles | Foreground props | Hotspots |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bedroom | 170 | tile id 1 ×120 (70.6%) | 10 | 9 placed tiles | 5 entities |
| Playground | 170 | tile id 3 ×120 (70.6%) | 10 | 11 placed tiles | 6 entities |

Both rooms are structurally identical 17×10 grids in which a wood/carpet floor tile is stamped 120 times, perimeter wall tiles fill the rest, and 9-11 "prop tiles" (a bed silhouette, a CRT silhouette, a phone silhouette) are dropped into the foreground layer. No layering. No perspective. No depth cues. No environmental storytelling. No negative space (the entire floor is uniform). No visual hierarchy directing the eye to interactives. No foreground/background separation — both layers are tile sprites at z=0 and z=10 with no actual parallax or staging.

Classification per audit spec: **`debug_playground`**. The rooms exist to test that LDtk loads, that entity layers iterate, that hotspot triggers fire. They were never authored as places.

PWNGLOVE Mode (`Playground`) is worse than Bedroom because it carries narrative weight — it's the demo-video showcase room — but visually it's a 408×240 carpet rectangle with 11 silhouette icons scattered along its perimeter and floor. Compare to the room-overview concept at `docs/concepts/playground_room_overview.png` (which shows depth, station differentiation, three-quarter perspective, scene composition): the shipped Playground is unrecognizable as the same room.

## 6. Animation findings (system vs art)

The animation **SYSTEM** is correct. AnimatedSprite is properly vendored; Phase 9 sprite migration cleanly loads addState from `animations_manifest.newb`; frame ranges match the imagetable layout; tickStep timings (30 idle, 6 walk) are reasonable; the FSM correctly switches state on d-pad input via `updateMovement`.

The animation **ART** is broken:
- Source frames are blurry smudges (see §4) — no amount of correct frame timing redeems unreadable source pixels.
- Idle states are single-frame holds for N/E/W after the v0.1.17 sprite-sync revert; only south has a 2-frame breathing variant — and those two frames differ by sub-pixel dither noise, not authored breath motion.
- Walk cycles do not register as walks because adjacent frames differ by less pixel motion than the per-frame ordered-dither noise floor.
- No transition animations (interact, surprised) read distinctly. `interact` is frame 25, `surprised` is frame 26 — both are head-close-up cells from the concept sheet that got downscaled to 32×32 and now read identically.

**Verdict:** ~5% system problem (no per-state breathing for 3/4 directions, no anchor validation in Newb wrapper), ~95% art problem. Re-doing addState code accomplishes nothing without replacement art.

## 7. LDtk findings

`source/levels/world.ldtk` (LDtk format 1.5.3, single-file, non-external levels) is being used as **layout scaffolding**, not as final art:

- Tiles are repeated obviously (70% floor uniformity per room).
- 24×24 tile size is on the small end for Playdate — at 1:1 ratio, 24×24 occupies 6% of screen width. Hand-detailed tiles at that size require pixel-art discipline the procedural builder doesn't apply.
- Tiles are visually flat — no shading, no perspective, no isometric depth. The builder `tools/levels/build_tileset.sh` outputs primitive shapes (rectangles, circles, text labels) dithered to 1-bit. The "shading" comes from the ordered-dither pass on solid fills, not from authored highlight/shadow.
- Object placement (entity layer) is grid-aligned with no compositional intent. The Tyson cabinet is a hotspot at LDtk grid (X, Y); there is no visual cluster of arcade chrome around it, no neon glow, no positional logic — just a labeled rectangle on a carpet.
- Tile layer ordering IS correct (Background z=0, Foreground z=10) — that's the only thing LDtk got right.
- Dithering is coming from auto-conversion (`-ordered-dither o4x4 -monochrome` on the tileset PNG), not from artist intent. The tileset itself is ImageMagick `-draw` primitives passed through the dither at the very end of the build script.

LDtk's role should be to compose authored tiles into authored rooms. Here it's composing primitive squares into primitive grids.

## 8. Image conversion findings

`tools/sprites/build_newb_from_concept.sh`, `tools/levels/build_tileset.sh`, `tools/launcher/build_launcher_assets.sh` — all use the same pipeline:

```
convert <source> -resize <target>^ -gravity center -extent <target>
  -filter point -ordered-dither o4x4 -monochrome
  -define png:bit-depth=1 -define png:color-type=0 <output>
```

Problems by line:

- `-resize 32x32^` then `-extent 32x32` on a 100×60 concept-art cell is a **lossy downscale of 90%**. Detail finer than 3 source pixels is gone. Outlines, eyes, hands, hood seams: all unrenderable post-resize.
- `-filter point` (nearest-neighbor) is the correct filter for pixel art *that was authored at the target resolution*. Applied to a downscale-by-3.1x of organic concept art, it samples sparse pixels and produces aliased blob shapes.
- `-ordered-dither o4x4` on a small image with limited tonal range produces visible 4×4 mesh patterns that look like Bayer-pattern artifacts, not authored dithering. Hand-pixel artists pick dither patterns per surface (GFXP has 136 of them for exactly this reason). The build script picks one and applies it globally.
- `-monochrome` after dither is a no-op safety (the dither already produced 1-bit) but confirms the asset will be 1-bit grayscale on disk — which is what the validator then "passes."
- `-define png:bit-depth=1 -define png:color-type=0` ensures the PNG file is stored as 1-bit indexed grayscale. Playdate-compatible. This is correct.
- No `-alpha remove` flag on the launcher script. Transparent pixels may produce artifacts when pdc converts to .pdi.

**The conversion is mechanically correct and aesthetically destructive.** It is the right toolchain applied at the wrong stage of the pipeline (downscaling concept art instead of receiving hand-pixel art at native target resolution).

## 9. Canon migration side effects

This is the load-bearing problem.

The Phase 1-14 migration locked all current state into the canon contract:

- `source/data/assets.lua` declares `priority='critical'` on `newb_table` (the procedural sprite), `tileset_hakcd_table_24_24` (the primitive tileset), and `lockpick_body` (the rectangle-with-bars UI). The validator confirms these resolve on disk. They're now official.
- `source/data/rooms.lua` declares both `sc01` (Bedroom) and `playground` as `is_story_room` with `ldtk_level` references. The 120-floor-tile-stamp layout is the canonical room shape.
- `source/data/animations.lua` declares the 10 newb states with frame ranges. The frame ranges are correct; the frames inside them are blobs. The manifest does not know this.
- `tools/canon/validate_continuity.sh` (the 9-check validator at Phase 7) verifies every id resolves, every transition target exists, every animation frame index falls within asset frame_count, every dialogue speaker is canon. **It does not check that any asset looks like anything.**
- `tools/asset_validator.sh` runs three gates per PNG: 2-color rule, edge-pixel count ≥ 8, silhouette dump to `build/silhouettes/`. The silhouette dump is the only quality signal — and it has no human-in-the-loop step requiring someone to scan the dump directory.

**Net effect:** the architecture sprint promoted placeholder visual language to "official" status. The 21/21 PNG validator pass and 9/9 graph validator pass are both technically correct statements about a tree full of unfinished art. The pipeline now actively resists replacing placeholders because doing so requires bumping versions, regenerating manifests, and re-running validators — none of which solve "this still looks bad."

**The validator is theatrical** in the way Lucas Pope warned about: it proves the contract holds, not that the contract is worth holding.

## 10. Missing quality gates

Enumerated gates that should exist but don't:

1. **Sprite-size minimum-against-target** — no check that newb-table-32-32 (a player sprite) renders ≥ 1/6 of screen height. (Currently ~13% of screen height in pixel-perfect rendering, ~6% if accounting for visual weight.)
2. **Silhouette readability automated check** — `build/silhouettes/` directory is written but never reviewed. No `make silhouettes-review` step that lists pairs likely to collide.
3. **Visual review manifest** — no `tools/visual_signoff.json` listing every asset with a `human_reviewed: true/false` + `reviewer_handle` + `date` field.
4. **Screenshot comparison against reference** — no fixture PNGs in `tests/visual/` showing what each scene should look like, with a perceptual-diff check on build.
5. **Hardware-photo review** — no documented step requiring sideload + device photo capture + comparison against simulator render.
6. **Asset classification machine-enforced** — `assets.lua` has a `priority` field but no `art_status: final|placeholder|generated|wip` field that the build can read to reject placeholders past a release threshold.
7. **Placeholder rejection at build time** — no `make all` target that fails when any asset is classified `placeholder` AND pdxinfo version ≥ 0.2.0.
8. **Room composition checklist** — no per-room metadata declaring intended depth layers / focal point / negative-space ratio / unique-tile-count minimum.
9. **Frame-anchor validation** — no check that each addState's frames share a consistent character anchor (feet-bottom alignment, head-top alignment).
10. **Animation preview-sheet export** — no `make animation-previews` step exporting each character's full FSM as a contact-sheet GIF for visual review.
11. **Playdate screenshot capture review** — no simulator-automation step that loads each scene, sleeps 2 frames, captures `.pdscreen` files, and bundles them into a per-version visual-diff PDF.
12. **Style-guide rule enforcement** — `docs/concept_art_prompt.md` lists rules (2px outlines, three-quarter perspective, etc) that no validator can verify mechanically. Either the rules need machine-checkable proxies or human signoff needs to be a build gate.

## 11. Immediate recommendations (what to STOP doing now)

1. **Stop downscaling concept art to small target sizes.** The `tools/sprites/build_newb_from_concept.sh` pattern is producing the worst single asset in the build. Concept art is concept art; hand-pixel art is the deliverable.
2. **Stop generating tilesets via ImageMagick `-draw` primitives.** `tools/levels/build_tileset.sh` produces a tileset that reads as debug iconography. Either commission/author a real tileset or use an off-the-shelf 1-bit Playdate-targeted set (e.g. Kenney 1-bit pack) as a temporary upgrade.
3. **Stop using ordered-dither o4x4 as universal output filter.** It's destroying detail on small assets. GFXP has 136 patterns for a reason; pick per-surface intentionally.
4. **Stop calling primitive rectangles "the playground."** Either author the room as a single illustrated PNG background OR build a real tileset that supports composition. The current 120-floor-stamps is not a room.
5. **Stop letting `validator passes` substitute for `art reviewed`.** The 21/21 and 9/9 gates are theater unless paired with a human signoff step.

## 12. Proposed visual pipeline rebuild

Mirror the canon-first migration structure — declarative, validator-gated, layered:

```
Reference Library    docs/reference/<game-name>/<scene>.{png,md}
        ↓
Style Guide          docs/style_guide.md (line weight, dither patterns,
                     perspective rules, anchor rules, scale rules)
        ↓
Visual Spec          source/data/visual_spec.lua per asset:
                       art_status: final|placeholder|generated|wip
                       human_reviewed: bool + reviewer + date
                       target_dimensions: w×h
                       readability_min_pct_screen: float
                       silhouette_test_id: string
                       reference_image: docs/reference/...
        ↓
Asset Authoring      hand-pixel at TARGET resolution (NOT downscale from
                     concept). Reference image alongside in PR.
        ↓
Visual Validator     tools/canon/validate_visuals.sh
                       - rejects art_status=placeholder when version >= 0.2.0
                       - confirms target_dimensions match file dimensions
                       - confirms human_reviewed=true for critical assets
                       - silhouette-collision detector
        ↓
Build Gate           make all chain:
                       asset_validator.sh (pixel format)
                       validate_continuity.sh (id graph)
                       validate_visuals.sh (art quality contract) ← NEW
                       pdc
        ↓
Per-Release Review   tools/visual_review_checklist.md
                       sideload + device photo + signoff
```

## 14. Definition of done for visuals

Machine-checkable criteria for v0.2.0:

1. `source/data/visual_spec.lua` exists with one entry per shippable asset.
2. Every `priority='critical'` asset in `assets.lua` has `art_status='final'` AND `human_reviewed=true` in visual_spec.
3. No asset's actual dimensions differ from its declared `target_dimensions`.
4. Every silhouette in `build/silhouettes/` is referenced in `tools/visual_review_signoffs.md` with a `reviewed_at` date.
5. Newb sprite reads as a hooded person to a first-time viewer at hardware 1:1 (subjective — requires signoff entry).
6. Bedroom + Playground rooms each have ≥ 30 unique tiles in use (vs current 10) OR ship as authored full-screen background PNGs.
7. No asset path resolves to a primitive ImageMagick-`-draw` output (signed off as authored).
8. `make all` chain passes asset_validator + validate_continuity + validate_visuals + pdc.
9. Per-scene playdate.simulator screenshots match reference fixtures within perceptual threshold.
10. Hardware sideload + device-photo signoff captured for the v0.2.0 build.

## Why It Looks Bad

The game looks bad because the architecture sprint succeeded at making the contract real and the validator real, but the contract only specified pixel format and graph integrity — not whether any asset is intentionally authored, readable at hardware scale, or compositionally coherent. The shipped player sprite is a 100×60 concept-art cell lossily downscaled to 32×32 with `-filter point -ordered-dither o4x4 -monochrome`, which reads as a wax-melted thumbprint, not a character. Both rooms are 17×10 LDtk grids in which 70% of the cells are the same repeated floor tile and the foreground layer contains 9-11 silhouette icons. Canon migration then promoted all of this to `priority=critical` in `assets.lua`, locked it behind a validator gate that confirms files exist and pixels are 1-bit but cannot tell that nothing has been drawn. The 21/21 PNG validator pass and the 9/9 graph validator pass are both true statements about a tree full of placeholder art. The pipeline is operationally correct and aesthetically empty. The fix is not more code — it is hand-pixel art at native target resolution, a style guide enforced at build time via a third validator, and a human-in-the-loop signoff step that the build can read. Until those land, every future asset will keep slotting cleanly into a contract that has no opinion about whether the art is finished.
